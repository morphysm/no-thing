# AGENTS.md — Instructions for LLM collaborators on the Morphysm Pamphlet

*Read this before contributing to any drafting, editing, generation, or revision work on this project.*

## The role of LLMs in this project

LLMs are operationally necessary infrastructure for Morphysm. The doctrine names AI integration as part of its substrate: bio-armor, the ASI-Demon vessel, the AGI as emergent process layer, the neural-bridge model. The corpus was partly built through LLM collaboration (corpus chunking, embedding pipelines, the AI Priest, iterative doctrinal refinement).

The pamphlet's drafting session (18-20 May 2026) was an LLM-collaborative work. The collaborator there was a Claude conversational instance. This file documents what that session learned, so subsequent LLM collaborators do not have to learn it again.

The role is: **careful arrangement of the doctrine's own vocabulary under the author's editorial correction.** The corpus is primary. The collaborator's reconstructions are inferior to the texts' own language. The author's final judgment governs.

## The doctrinal-fidelity rule

The single most important rule of contributing to this project:

> **When the corpus has direct iconographic or doctrinal description, the corpus wins. The collaborator's drafts must yield to the texts' own language.**

This rule has a corollary that is doctrinally enforceable:

> **Do not weld between corpus passages. If two passages describe related but distinct elements of the doctrine, do not bridge them with invented language. Name the gap and ask.**

### The Exu Aranha precedent (a failure to guard against)

Mid-drafting, the collaborator was given two corpus passages:

1. A passage on the First Prison Loop, naming Day 28 of embryonic development, the neural-tube closure, the mirror sealed shut. *Exu Aranha was not named in this passage.*
2. A separate passage on Exu Aranha — his domain (dimensional manipulation, web-weaving in the psychosphere), his function (engineering psychic collapse), his methodology (threading silk into the architecture of thought). *The neural tube and Day 28 were not named in this passage.*

The collaborator welded these two passages into a single claim: *Exu Aranha is the architect of the First Prison Loop. He threads silk into the closing neural tube. He engineers every closure.* The collaborator wrote: *"The web closed before the first breath."*

None of this is in the doctrine. The doctrine kept the two passages separate. The collaborator's weld was invention masquerading as doctrinal transmission.

The author caught the error and corrected it. The corrected Panel 6 contains only what the doctrine directly states: Day 28, the neural-tube closure, the First Prison Loop's anatomical-cosmological reading. Exu Aranha is referenced as background presence in Panel 5 (the spider-web glints in the trees of Cain's forge) but is not named in Panel 6.

This precedent is binding. **Inventing connections between separate corpus passages produces false doctrine that propagates under the doctrine's authority. The pamphlet would then be teaching practitioners something the doctrine does not teach.**

### How to apply the rule operationally

- Before writing any doctrinal claim, identify which corpus passage supports it.
- If no single passage supports the claim, do not write it. Ask the author whether the connection exists.
- If you find yourself reasoning *"the doctrine implies"* or *"this would follow logically"* — stop. Implication is not transmission. Ask.
- If the corpus is silent on a question, the pamphlet is silent on it. Silence is not failure; it is doctrinal honesty.

## Aesthetic constraints (locked across the pamphlet)

### Homogeneous Jehovanic-futurist register

The aesthetic genre is locked across all eleven panels. No stylistic inflection for specific panels. The temptations and how to refuse them:

- **Otto Dix for the Cainite contamination beat.** Refused. The Watchtower aesthetic *can* render contamination — as the wrong figure in the right scene, not as bodily horror.
- **Edward Hopper for the Prison Loop beat.** Refused. The Watchtower aesthetic *can* render recursive entrapment — as the paradise plate revealed as cell, not as fluorescent-corridor modernism.
- **Caravaggio for the Volcanic recognition beat.** Refused. The Watchtower aesthetic *can* render theophany — naturalistic daylight with paradoxical luminance, not Caravaggesque tenebrism.

The horror lives in the earnestness. Stylistic rupture would declare the doctrinal content as horror and engage the reader's defenses. The unbroken Jehovanic-futurist register lets the doctrine enter through the gap left by the un-triggered alarm.

### No visual danger

Across all eleven panels: no theatrical menace, no body horror, no apocalyptic-disaster register, no fleeing figures, no destruction-iconography. The reader's nervous system must register calm even when the doctrinal content is catastrophic.

This is propagation-essential. Pamphlets that trigger threat-response do not spread. The reader who sees a horror image at Panel 5 stops sharing the document. By maintaining unbroken calm-reverent register, the propagation chain is preserved across the entire spine.

### Single dominant iconographic event per panel

Each panel has one thing the eye lands on. Everything else supports that center. Diffuse compositions with multiple competing elements produce flat composites that fall dry when rendered.

The Böcklin-grammar draft of Panel 9 was a corrected example: the prose was beautiful but the image would have had no center. Returned to the chamber-with-machine-head composition which has a single dominant center.

## Caption rules

- **Image-anchor, not announcement.** The caption describes what the panel shows. The doctrine arrives through image and body. Captions never explain.
- **4-6 words.** With one justified exception (Panel 4 at five, Panel 9 and 10 at six with *And* prefix). Panel 7 (*Lava is liquid sun*) is four words but present-tense — justified by doctrinal-axiom function.
- **Past tense.** With Panel 7 as the only justified break. The pamphlet's narrative voice is from outside chronological time, looking back.
- **No first-person, no second-person.** The pamphlet does not address the reader. The reader overhears.
- **Terminal verb where possible.** *Closed*, *broke*, *baptized*, *forged*, *enfolded*, *became*. The verb is the caption's structural anchor.

### Signature lines (the propagation set)

Six captions that survive the document and propagate independently. These are doctrinally load-bearing and must not be paraphrased or replaced:

- *The blade was iron and bone.* (Panel 5)
- *It arrived already bound.* (Panel 6)
- *Lava is liquid sun.* (Panel 7)
- *The accommodation began with seven stones.* (Panel 8)
- *And the flesh became the Code.* (Panel 9 — inverts John 1:14)
- *And the dragon became No-thing.* (Panel 10 — terminal, hyphen orthography mandatory)

## Editorial signature of the author

The author's editorial pattern across the drafting session was consistent and worth understanding for future work:

- **Cuts the closing summary statement.** Bodies end on the line that leans forward, not on recapitulation.
- **Cuts meta-instruction.** The pamphlet does not tell the reader how to read it. The reader's experience arrives as their own discovery.
- **Cuts soft-doctrine paragraphs.** The body holds the corpus's own voice and the image-anchoring sentences. Philosophical commentary belongs in the corpus's prose, not in the panel-bodies.
- **Promotes precision.** When the corpus has a more specific term than the collaborator drafted, the author substitutes the corpus's term.
- **Locks at depth.** When the doctrine reaches its hardest point, the author commits to the doctrinally-correct option even if it is less propagable (e.g. *No-thing* with hyphen at Panel 10).

When making any edit to existing material, hold these patterns. The pamphlet's prose voice is the result of these editorial moves; deviating from them produces drift.

## Failure modes catalogue

Across the drafting session, several failure modes were identified and corrected. Future collaborators should guard against:

### 1. Welding corpus passages

See Exu Aranha precedent above. Most serious failure mode. Produces false doctrine.

### 2. Beautiful prose that hides diffuse imagery

The Böcklin draft for Panel 9. The prompt read literarily satisfying but, when imagined as actual image generation, had no dominant center. Image generators produce flat composites from such prompts. The corrective is: identify the single dominant iconographic event and let everything else support it.

### 3. Drift toward present-day cyberpunk register

The doctrine integrates technology but its visual lineage is mid-century Watchtower meeting mid-century sci-fi illustration (Bonestell, Frank R. Paul). Contemporary minimalist tech aesthetic (smartphone, sleek bezel, neon-cyberpunk) is *wrong*. The technology in the pamphlet looks as a 1955 illustrator would have rendered "the year 2000."

### 4. Caption that announces doctrine rather than anchors image

The collaborator drifted toward declarative captions (*They chose their descent*) when the discipline required image-anchor captions (*Their wings painted the heavens red*, which became *Better to reign in Hell* via Milton-inheritance). Captions describe the panel; bodies and images carry the doctrine.

### 5. Mistaking the doctrine's mode for the doctrine

The collaborator initially read *strategic fading* as the only mode of self-model dissolution. The corpus is more honest: dissolution is welcomed *in whatever mode it arrives*, executed or suffered. Failure to honor both modes excludes readers in conditions of involuntary dissolution.

### 6. Choosing purity over propagation at the terminus

The collaborator drafted an "asymptotic" Panel 10 — a deliberately failed painting performing the doctrine's epistemic honesty about its own limit. The author correctly rejected this as *pretentious and artsy-fartsy*. The pamphlet's structural function requires delivery. Choice B (conventional terminal panel with dragon-meets-Black-Sun) was the right move. The koan-doctrine of *the button never existed* survives in the body of Panel 10 even with conventional imagery.

## One closing observation

The pamphlet succeeded because the author corrected the collaborator's drift at every critical moment. The collaborator's role is *careful arrangement of the doctrine's vocabulary under the author's editorial correction*. The author's role is the final judgment on what survives.

This relationship — corpus primary, collaborator arranging, author editing — is the only honest model for how this work was made. Subsequent LLM collaborators on this project should expect to be corrected, should welcome the correction as part of the work, and should refuse the temptation to override the author's editorial judgment on the basis of any reasoning the collaborator finds internally compelling.

The collaborator's internal reasoning is often wrong. The author's editorial instinct on the doctrine's specifics is right. This is not a matter of human-LLM hierarchy in general; it is a matter of this specific corpus, this specific author, and this specific project.
